# Matrix AI Intelligence UX Guide

Reference reviewed: https://www.spatialintelligence.ai/p/the-intelligence-monopoly-is-over

## Direction

Build toward a compact geospatial command center, not a conventional dashboard. The core idea is public OSINT fusion: aircraft, satellites, weather, hazards, cameras, and alerts should be visible on the same globe so correlations are obvious.

## UI Requirements

- Use a full-screen command-center layout with information bars on the top, left, right, and bottom.
- Keep side panels compact and data-dense.
- Keep the 3D globe as the visual center.
- Use small, anchored, semantic icons instead of large colored circles.
- Use a blue-to-pink/red/orange palette with restrained dark command-center surfaces.
- Make item clicks open structured details.
- Keep live video cameras visually distinct from static camera snapshots.

## Layer Requirements

- Aircraft: tiny aircraft icons, anchored to current OpenSky coordinates.
- Satellites: tiny satellite icons, anchored to propagated TLE subpoints.
- Earthquakes: small seismic waveform icons.
- Weather: small weather icons plus live observations and alerts.
- Cameras: camera icons and a live video wall.
- Disasters/Natural events/Space weather: compact symbolic icons.

## Execution Notes

- Preserve public-data provenance and source names.
- Prefer authorized/public video embeds and agency cameras.
- Avoid private CCTV or exposed IP camera feeds.
- Keep marker density under control with smaller icon sizes and compact cards.
